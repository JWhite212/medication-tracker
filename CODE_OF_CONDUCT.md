# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Everyone who contributes to or interacts with this project — maintainers,
contributors, and visitors — commits to keeping the collaboration space
respectful, professional, and inclusive. The aim is a small, healthy
project where good ideas can come from anyone and where people feel safe
participating regardless of background, experience level, or identity.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful in every interaction, including disagreements.
- Give feedback that is specific, constructive, and focused on the work.
- Receive feedback graciously; assume the reviewer is trying to help.
- Centre conversations on the technical merits of the contribution.
- Assume good faith — most misunderstandings are easier to fix than to
  litigate.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory language directed at any
  contributor.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, reviews, or issue threads.
- Sharing someone's private information (email, address, employer, etc.)
  without their explicit consent.

## Enforcement

The maintainer reviews concerns case by case and uses judgement
proportional to the issue. Responses can include a private warning, a
request to revise a comment or PR, closing the contribution, or removing
access to project spaces. Severe or repeated violations are escalated
through GitHub's reporting tools.

## Reporting

If something happens that you'd like the maintainer to look at, please
open a private GitHub Security Advisory on this repository, or open a
GitHub issue and mark it private. Reports are handled discreetly.

## Scope

This code applies everywhere the project lives — issues, pull requests,
discussions, commit messages, and any other space tied to the repository.
